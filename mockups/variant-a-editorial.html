I was a registered nurse in India for 5 years. I moved to Toronto 8 months ago and have no idea what to do next.
That's a solid foundation — 5 years of clinical experience is genuinely valuable here. Where did you complete your nursing degree, and was it a 3-year diploma or a 4-year BSc?
4-year BSc Nursing from Mumbai University, completed 2019. I worked on a medical-surgical ward at Lilavati Hospital.
Great — BSc from Mumbai University plus med-surg experience gives you real options in Toronto. Here are three realistic paths based on what you've told me:
01

PSW Fast Entry

3–6 months
Steps
  1. Enroll in an Ontario-approved PSW certificate program at George Brown or Humber (approx. $2,000–$3,500, part-time options)
  2. Complete the clinical placement hours required by the program
  3. Apply to long-term care homes, home care agencies, or Toronto hospitals — your nursing background makes you a standout
  4. Begin earning while continuing RN recertification steps in parallel
Certifications
  • PSW Certificate (Ontario-approved program)
  • BLS (already complete)
Realistic salary
$38,000 – $50,000 CAD
02

RN Recertification via CNO & NNAS

18–24 months
Steps
  1. Submit your nursing credentials to NNAS (National Nursing Assessment Service) — fee approx. $650 CAD
  2. Register for a WES ECA evaluation for your BSc from Mumbai University
  3. Apply to the College of Nurses of Ontario (CNO) once the NNAS advisory report is complete
  4. Prepare for and write the NCLEX-RN exam (approx. $360 USD registration fee)
  5. Consider an Ontario college IEN bridging program (Centennial or Humber offer these) to fill any identified gaps — fits within your $3–5k budget
Certifications
  • NNAS Advisory Report
  • CNO Registration
  • NCLEX-RN
Realistic salary
$70,000 – $88,000 CAD
03

Clinical Research Coordinator

6–12 months
Steps
  1. Complete a free or low-cost Good Clinical Practice (GCP) certification online through CITI Program
  2. Take a short Clinical Research Associate/Coordinator course — Toronto Metropolitan University and some Ontario colleges offer these under $3,000
  3. Tailor your resume to highlight your med-surg clinical knowledge and patient assessment skills, which are valued in research
  4. Target Toronto's hospital research institutes (UHN, Sinai Health, SickKids) and contract research organizations (CROs) for coordinator roles
Certifications
  • GCP Certification (CITI Program)
  • Optional: SOCRA or ACRP Clinical Research Coordinator certification later
Realistic salary
$52,000 – $70,000 CAD
